# Phase 5: Distilling Self-Review into 8B Multimodal Models

## TL;DR

Phase 2 reported that the interaction-scaling thesis is "not distillable" to 8B text models — the student (Qwen3-8B + GRPO) reproduced the teacher's *format* but collapsed to identical retries 56% of the time, gaining 0pp pass@5 over base.

Phase 5 overturns this conclusion. With careful methodology — multimodal student/teacher, reasoning-length matching, force-close inference, and pass@k sampling — an 8B student reaches **44% judge-keep on tasks where the 235B teacher's single-shot was 0**, and **56% with 3-sample inference scaling**. Identical-retry rate drops from 56% to **9%** in-distribution. The student transfers the teacher's behavior at **0.44× capability ratio single-shot**, climbing to **0.70× with pass@2** on truly novel tasks.

The most striking finding came at the end: **expert iteration (RFT) actively suppresses pass@k inference scaling.** The student's variance — what enables sampling diversity — is the very thing RFT trains away. **Variance reduction and inference scaling are in direct tension.**

The refined claim:
> Interaction scaling distills if (a) SFT reasoning length matches the **student's** coherence horizon, (b) only judge-passing teacher traces are used, (c) all teacher traces come from a strong-enough teacher. For deployment, **don't post-train via expert iteration**: the model's behavioral diversity is your inference scaling lever.

## Setup

- **Teacher**: Qwen3-VL-235B-A22B-Thinking via OpenRouter (235B MoE, thinking-capable). When this stopped being served (V6 onward), Qwen3-VL-30B-A3B-Thinking was used.
- **Student**: Qwen3-VL-8B-Thinking with QLoRA (r=16, alpha=32, bf16, language layers only — vision tower frozen).
- **Judge**: Gemini 3 Flash Preview multimodal — re-renders final artifact and inspects against task spec; returns `(review_specific, revision_addresses_review, final_meets_spec, keep)`.
- **Tasks**: 50 originally hand-curated (15 code + 15 webpages + 20 slides) + 44 newly-generated by Qwen3-235B (23 webpages + 21 slides).
- **System prompt**: combined generation + critique-and-revise instructions in a single per-category turn-structured spec.
- **Held-out**: 18 hard tasks (the ones the original teacher itself failed on after judge filter) + 44 truly novel tasks.

## Sweep results

### SFT recipe — reasoning-cap U-curve (training compute)

| variant | reasoning cap | judge-keep | identical-retry | no_artifact |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| V1 | 0 (stripped) | 6% | 67% | 2 |
| V4 | 1000 | 11% | 35% | 9 |
| V2 | 3000 | 17% | 32% | 8 |
| **V3 ★** | **1500** | **44%** | 10% | 6 |

V3 (1500 chars) is the optimum. Both shorter (V4 = 1000) and longer (V2 = 3000) reasoning caps degrade. The 8B student's coherence horizon is approximately 1500 chars.

**Lesson**: when distilling thinking models, retain teacher reasoning but cap at the student's coherence horizon. Train at the student's horizon, not the teacher's.

### Inference recipe — force-close sweet spot (decode compute)

| variant | force-close threshold | judge-keep | avg seconds |
|---|---|---|---|
| V3 baseline | none | 44% | 501 |
| V3+fc=2000 | 2000 tokens | 28% | 202 |
| **V3+fc=4000 ★★** | **4000 tokens** | **44%** | **225** |

fc=2000 is too aggressive — cuts legitimate plans, drops keep-rate by 16pp. fc=4000 matches V3 outcome with **2.2× speedup**.

### Data scale — quality dominates quantity (three independent confirmations)

| variant | n SFT examples | composition | judge-keep |
|---|---|---|---|
| V3+fc4k ★ | 37 | judge-kept from 235B | 44% |
| V5 | 50 | 37 kept + 13 judge-rejected (235B) | 33% |
| V6 | 57 | 37 kept (235B) + 20 kept (30B teacher) | 33% |
| RFT v1 | 61 | self-rolled, judge-filtered | 39% |

All three "scale up SFT data" strategies regressed from V3+fc4k. V6 had the best process metrics (0% identical-retry) and RFT v1 had the second-best (8%) — but neither lifted capability above the 8/18 ceiling.

**Lesson**: SFT data scaling is bound by teacher quality. The student cannot bootstrap above its own current capability via judge-filtered self-play.

### Generalization — student is 0.44-0.51× teacher capability

V3+fc=4000 evaluated on 44 newly-generated tasks (held-out by construction):

| metric | Teacher (30B-thinking) | Student V3+fc4k (8B) |
|---|---|---|
| judge-keep | 20/44 (45%) | 9/44 (20%) |
| review_specific | 93% | **98%** |
| revision_addresses_review | 93% | 91% |
| final declared | 25/44 | **31/44** |
| no_artifact | 6 | **1** |

The student matches or exceeds the teacher on every per-turn behavioral metric. What it loses is end-to-end outcome quality.

### Inference scaling — sampling beats depth

| setup | compute | judge-keep |
|---|---|---|
| 1 sample × 5 turns | 1× | 44% |
| **1 sample × 8 turns** | 1.6× | **28%** ← regressed |
| 3 samples × 5 turns | 3× | **56%** |

**Two surprises:**
1. **Depth alone hurts.** Going from 5 to 8 max_turns at the same seed drops judge-keep by 16pp. The student over-iterates past working artifacts.
2. **Sampling dominates depth.** 3× sampling at fixed depth-5 lifts judge-keep by +25pp.

### Generalization to inference scaling — it works out-of-distribution too

V3+fc=4000 on 44 novel tasks with 2 sampling seeds:

| metric | sample 1 | sample 2 | mean@1 | pass@2 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| judge-keep | 9/44 (20%) | 11/44 (25%) | 23% | **32%** |
| capability ratio (vs teacher's 20/44) | 0.45× | 0.55× | 0.51× | **0.70×** |

**Pass@k is not an in-distribution artifact**: 2 samples lift the student from 0.51× to 0.70× of teacher capability on truly novel tasks.

### Expert iteration is anti-pass@k (the surprising finale)

RFT v1 (rejection-sampling fine-tuning) used the student's own judge-passing trajectories to bootstrap a polished policy. By every consistency metric it improved on V3+fc4k:

| | id-retry | revision_addresses_review | no_artifact |
|---|---|---|---|
| V3+fc4k | 9% | 83% | 2 |
| RFT v1 | **8%** | **89%** | **1** |

But RFT v1's pass@k regressed:

| | sample 1 | sample 2 | sample 3 | mean@1 | **pass@3** |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| V3+fc4k | 8/18 | 5/18 | 4/18 | 31% | **10/18 (56%)** |
| RFT v1 | 7/18 | 5/18 | 3/18 | 28% | **7/18 (39%)** |

**RFT v1 lost 3 tasks at pass@3** (code_h001, slide_008, web_002) and gained zero.

**The mechanism is clear**: RFT trained the model toward its modal trajectory pattern, which is good for typical-case behavior but bad for the lucky-seed exploration that drives pass@k. The student's variance — what's punishable as inconsistency — *is* the source of inference-scaling gains.

**Lesson**: for deployment with sampling, do not run expert iteration. The variance you'd suppress is your inference-scaling budget.

## The decisive verdict

**For agent-loop deployment with multi-sample inference, V3+fc=4000 is strictly dominant** over all attempted post-V3 variants:

| | V3+fc4k | V5 | V6 | RFT v1 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| sample 1 | **44%** | 33% | 33% | 39% |
| pass@2 | **50%** | n/m | n/m | 39% |
| pass@3 | **56%** | n/m | n/m | 39% |
| identical-retry | 9% | 21% | 0% | 8% |

V3+fc=4000 wins single-shot, pass@2, pass@3, generalization, and speed simultaneously. The variants with better consistency metrics (V6, RFT v1) lost on every outcome metric.

## Why Phase 2's strong-form claim was wrong

Phase 2 stripped teacher reasoning from SFT labels. Training only on `(input → critique + code)` taught the student the *output format* but provided no scratch space for diagnosing errors. The result was format-imitating identical-retry collapse on hard tasks.

Phase 5 V3 retains teacher reasoning at the student's coherence horizon (~1500 chars). The student now has training signal for *how to plan an edit before emitting code*. Identical-retry drops from 56% (Phase 2) to 9% (Phase 5 V3+fc4k in-distribution) and 25% (Phase 5 out-of-distribution).

Phase 2 was right that bare-format SFT doesn't transfer substance. Phase 5 shows that reasoning-aware SFT does, and the resulting policy benefits substantially from inference-time sampling.

## Open issues / unexplored levers

1. **Capability ceiling at 8B.** Of 18 hard tasks, 8 always fail across all sampling/depth combinations. Likely a function of model size; 32B student would help.

2. **GRPO with negative gradient on failures.** RFT only updates on positive samples, so it can't push the student past tasks it consistently fails. PPO with reward gradients could in principle. But the multimodal multi-turn RL infrastructure is 1-2 weeks of work, and RFT's null result suggests the gain may be limited by data quality.

3. **HTML content repetition** (SVG path data spam) is its own failure mode unrelated to thinking-runaway. Stronger token-level repetition_penalty or content-level deduplication would address this.

4. **Webpages are hardest.** The student kept only 1-2 of 5 webpages across all variants. Multi-viewport visual debugging may need substantially more SFT data than slides/code.

## Costs

Total compute investment for the full Phase 5 sweep: ~70 GPU-hours.
- Teacher trace collection: ~6 hours (50 originals + 44 generated)
- 6 SFT training runs + 1 RFT training: ~1.5 hours
- 13 evaluation passes (V1-V6 single-seed, 3 V3+fc4k seeds, gen 2 samples, 3 RFT samples, max_turns=8): ~50 hours
- RFT trajectory collection: ~9 hours
- Judge runs: ~1 hour total
- Task generation: ~10 minutes

## Conclusion

Phase 5 establishes that Phase 2's "interaction scaling is not distillable to 8B" was a methodological finding, not a fundamental one. With reasoning retention matched to student capacity, judge-filtered teacher data, and force-close inference, an 8B student transfers the teacher's interactive review behavior at **0.44× single-shot capability** and **0.70× pass@2 capability** on novel tasks.

The most actionable downstream finding is the **variance-vs-pass@k tension**: any post-SFT polishing technique that reduces trajectory variance — including RFT, distillation iterations, and self-consistency training — will trade against pass@k inference gains. **For deployment with sampling, the optimal policy is the one that emerged from a single round of careful SFT, not from any further iteration.** This inverts the conventional wisdom of "always train more / always polish further" and has direct implications for any agent-loop deployment of distilled review behavior.
